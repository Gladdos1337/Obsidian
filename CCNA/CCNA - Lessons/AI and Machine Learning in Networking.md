## Fundamentals

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: Systems that simulate human-like intelligence to recognize patterns, learn, make decisions, and solve problems.
    
- **Machine Learning (ML)**: A subset of AI where algorithms identify patterns in data to improve performance without explicit, hard-coded programming.
    
- **Deep Learning**: A subset of ML using multi-layered artificial neural networks to process complex, unstructured datasets.
    

## Machine Learning Types

- **Supervised Learning**: Model is trained on labeled data (inputs with known outcomes) to learn relationships and predict labels for new data.
    
- **Unsupervised Learning**: Model analyzes unlabeled data to find hidden structures, patterns, or groupings (clustering).
    
- **Reinforcement Learning**: An agent learns through trial and error within an environment, using a system of rewards and penalties to optimize actions.
    

## Predictive vs. Generative AI

- **Predictive AI**: Analyzes historical data to forecast future trends or anomalies.
    
    - **Networking Applications**: Traffic forecasting, threat detection, and predictive maintenance.
        
- **Generative AI**: Uses ML to create new content (text, images, or code) based on patterns in existing data.
    
    - **Networking Applications**: Generating configurations, troubleshooting scripts, and automated documentation.
        

## Cisco Catalyst Center AI Features

Cisco integrates AI to move from reactive troubleshooting to proactive network assurance.

- **AI Network Analytics**: Defines dynamic baselines for "normal" network behavior and identifies deviations (anomalies) that static thresholds might miss.
    
- **Machine Reasoning Engine (MRE)**: Uses an automated knowledge base to perform root-cause analysis and suggest specific remediation steps for network issues.
    
- **AI Endpoint Analytics**: Uses ML to identify and profile devices based on traffic patterns, ensuring visibility for IoT and unauthorized hardware.
    
- **AI-enhanced RRM (Radio Resource Management)**: Optimizes wireless environments by adjusting signals to account for interference and client density.
    

## Key CCNA Concepts

- **Reactive vs. Proactive**: Traditional networking is reactive (fixing things after they break). AI-driven networking is proactive (predicting and preventing issues).
    
- **Controller-Based Processing**: Because AI/ML models require significant compute power, these functions are typically performed by the network controller (e.g., Catalyst Center) rather than individual switches or routers.
    
- **Data Importance**: The accuracy of AI/ML in networking depends on the volume and quality of telemetry data collected from the infrastructure.
